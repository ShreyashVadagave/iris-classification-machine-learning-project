1. Iris Setosa
Description: Iris Setosa is known for its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in early spring.
Habitat: It is commonly found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams.
Color: The flowers are usually a vibrant blue or purple, with distinctive white markings on the petals.
Interesting Fact: Iris Setosa is highly adaptable and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking for low-maintenance plants.
2. Iris Versicolor
Description: Also known as the Blue Flag Iris, Iris Versicolor features larger flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
Habitat: This species prefers moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes.
Color: The flowers are typically blue to violet, with yellow and white markings that attract pollinators.
Interesting Fact: Iris Versicolor is the provincial flower of Quebec, Canada, and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance.
3. Iris Virginica
Description: Iris Virginica, commonly referred to as the Southern Blue Flag, has tall, elegant flowers that bloom in late spring.
Habitat: It thrives in wetland areas, including marshes and riverbanks, and is often found in the southeastern United States.
Color: The flowers are usually a deep blue or purple, with intricate patterns that vary among individual plants.
Interesting Fact: Iris Virginica is not only appreciated for its beauty but also plays a crucial role in supporting local wildlife, providing habitat for various species of birds and insects.
